Sentriq System Status

This page describes how Sentriq communicates service availability, what our uptime commitments are, and where to look during an incident. For the live, real-time status of each component, see the status page linked from your workspace footer.

Current commitments

Sentriq targets 99.9% monthly uptime for the core application and API. Enterprise agreements may include a contractual SLA with service credits; see your order form for specifics.

Components we track

Component What it covers
Web application The Sentriq dashboard and all in-app workflows
Public API REST API used by integrations and customer scripts
Continuous Monitoring The 24-hour control re-check engine
Integrations sync Pulling configuration data from connected systems
Trust Center Public, customer-hosted trust pages
Notifications Slack and email delivery for findings and alerts

Each component is reported independently, so a delay in integrations sync does not show the whole platform as down.

Status levels

  • Operational — the component is fully functional.
  • Degraded performance — the component works but is slow or partially impaired (for example, monitoring runs are delayed).
  • Partial outage — a subset of customers or one capability is affected.
  • Major outage — the component is unavailable for most customers.
  • Maintenance — planned work, announced in advance.

During an incident

When we open an incident you will see:

  1. A banner in the app pointing to the status page.
  2. Regular updates on the status page (typically every 30–60 minutes until resolved).
  3. A post-incident summary for major outages, including root cause and the corrective actions we are taking.

Subscribe on the status page to get incident notifications by email or webhook.

Planned maintenance

We schedule maintenance during low-traffic windows and announce it at least 48 hours ahead on the status page. Continuous Monitoring runs are queued during maintenance and resume automatically — no monitoring data is lost, though a 24-hour re-check may be deferred to the next window.

Does an outage affect my compliance evidence?

No. Evidence already collected is stored durably and is unaffected by an application outage. If a monitoring run is delayed by an incident, the affected controls are re-checked on the next cycle and any gap is visible in the control's history.

Reporting a problem

If you believe Sentriq is down but the status page shows operational, contact support@sentriq.site or use in-app chat. Enterprise customers can escalate through their dedicated CSM or Slack Connect channel.
